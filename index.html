Registration Template

Use this Google Sheets template to plan out class schedules for the upcoming semester!

link here
Instructions:
  1. Go to file -> Make a copy and save it to a location you can easily access
  2. Fill in your required classes in the sheet 'Requirements for Semester!"
  3. Look through the course catalog for courses/sections that meet the requirements
  4. Fill in each section with their times, professors etc in the respective columns in the 'Class Selection' sheet
  5. Make a schedule! Choose the specific sections for each required class and any electives and build a schedule!
  6. Fill in the sheet "schedule 1" with the course name and title and CRN in the respective columns!
  7. Make multiple schedules with DIFFERENT sections for each class, in case you do not get your preferred class times and schedules, make sure you have back ups! This will make registration much smoother.
  8. That's it! You're all set. Set your alarms for the day of registration and when it starts, go!

Finding (good) electives

...doesn't have to be difficult! If you take the time look through beforehand there are many options available. Below are a few tips on how to choose ones that you might enjoy!

Consider what you are interested in. It could be something you’ve done before, or something you’ve always wanted to try or learn more about!

Bearing that in mind, use the various filters in the course catalog to browse through available courses that might fit your interests. For classes you find interesting, you should note down their class code and CRN

eg. PSAM Subject code shows electives in printmaking, fine art, photography and illustration

Play around with major class timings and see what electives or classes would fit with your schedule for your core classes

Note!
Check if you need permission to register for some classes. Sometimes electives are limited by class level or having taken specific classes to register for them.

Tips!

Just some other things that might help you here and there in the process :-)

  1. Make an appointment with your advisor the week before registration to discuss your plans. Use Starfish to book your appointment as they fill up fast!
  2. Come prepared for your appointment with different class schedules to review!
  3. CHECK FOR HOLDS THE WEEK BEFORE! If you have a hold, you will not be able to choose your classes at your supposed time slot. Advisors are NOT able to register you for classes on their end and you will just have to make do with whatever classes or sections are left.
  4. Review your curriculum for your majors before looking at your class schedule– remember, classes for yourmajor should come before electives. You can always take some electives in another semester.
  5. Check your emails! Your ddvisors and the Registrars' Office will send you any updates there, including but not limited to your registration date & time, any passcodes etc.
  6. Choose professors whose work interests you and that you're excited to learn from, I find that it makes me look forward for class more or just makes classes more bearable in general. If you do get a class that is faculty TBA, keep an open mind!
